The Battle Royale Uprising

The first Battle Royale game could be traced back to a fanmade game in Minecraft called Minecraft Survival Games. This was a Minecraft spin-off to the popular novel: The Hunger Games. Its popularity among players would inspire modders to create battle royale mods for other video games. This would eventually lead to the creation of a game called Player Unknown's Battlegrounds. This game was a phenomenal success and would inspire a generation of battle royale games. Player Unknown's Battlegrounds would receive millions of daily players until it was challenged by a new battle royale. The addition of a battle royale mode to Epic Games' Fortnite proved to be a massive success, even succeeding Player Unknown's Battlegrounds in popularity. This brings us to the present, where many companies are attempting to "cash out" on the battle royale genre's success. There have been many battle royale games released since Fortnite: Battle Royale's success. However, none of these games have been popular enough to succeed the two big battle royale games mentioned. Instead, the quality of these games seem lower than their actual potential and the slew of battle royale knockoffs on the App Store and Google Play Store are even worse in terms of quality. It seems as though the current market is only focused on profits instead of quality.
