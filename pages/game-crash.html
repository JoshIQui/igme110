The Video Game Crash of 1983

The Video Game Crash of 1983 was caused by a massive wave of poor quality games in an attempt to profit off of the rapidly growing video game entertainment medium. As a result, people lost interest in video games due to the slew of awful, low quality video games that were being made. The prime culprit of the crash was Atari, who led the greedy market at the time. Their most infamous game during this time was E.T., which destroyed video game lovers' desires for video games. There is even a landfill of E.T. cartridges that were buried. It would take some time for the genre to heal, but this was accelerated by nintendo's entrance into the market with their Nintendo Entertainment System. With an incredibly strong line-up of video games, Nintendo was able to heal the market. The healing was further accelerated by the entrance of other companies into the market. Eventually, all was well in the video game market. However, the crash serves to remind us of the dangers of profit-chasing rather than consumer relationships and a care for quality.
